You open the internal door and find yourself inside a living room. Compared to the outside, this place feels a little more solid, more believable.
The walls are dark grey, and, if you are feeling cold, there is a fire going in the fireplace.


The mantelpiece above this has some strange objects on it, several photographs of ferrets, and a small bird inside a clock, who hoots at you and tells you the time. You try not to look too closely at the really unsettling...glossy... naked ceramic man.
A print of a frog looks down at you, very disapprovingly.


Next to the fireplace is a large shelf of board games. Interesting-looking ones, with really nice boxes.


There is a large corner sofa which has quite a lot of cushions and blankets on it. A small fox sits on the back, amongst other creatures, on his ear is a wooden peg, it has "you look like you need a good pegging" written on it.
Steady on, you think, raising your eyebrow. It reminds you of a field in Devon, and, weirdly, you feel an overwhelming desire to find some fish. Perhaps a passing holographic fairy might know where to find some, you catch yourself thinking. You faintly hear a song about drunken sailors (and what to do with them) playing from a mysterious source.


There is a television, connected to a computer, who tells you that his name is Cuboid Robert (Cubey Bob to his friends). You turn him on (he likes this, and whirrs his fans appreciatively).
On the desktop you can see a shortcut for Spotify. Actually, you can't, because shortcuts are ugly, and desktops should be as empty as possible. These things are pinned in the start menu instead.


There are two doors in this room, one looks like it leads into a kitchen, and the other must go into the tower that you noticed when you were outside.
